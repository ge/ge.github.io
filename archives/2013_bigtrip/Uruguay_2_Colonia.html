Colonia

Colonia turned out to be a human-scale city on a small point of land, jutting out into the river separating Uruguay from Argentina.

We checked into our room at Posada Del Rio. It was probably the worst room in town, with the beds at two levels to fit into the tiny room. It wasn't easy for David to climb up and down, using the chair as a ladder.



It didn't take much walking to see the tiny old city, climb the lighthouse, and see the other sights of this beautiful, human-scale town.

We sat on a bench with a beautiful view of the water and had David's chimarrão.








We waited for a restaurant near the lighthouse to open but gave up when there was no sign of life at 6:30. David had spotted an ad for a craft brewery, which opened. We were unfashionably early customers at the Barbot Cerveceria. We spent the evening at the bar, drinking good beer and eating a nice dinner. David remembered Silvia's advice to try Panqueques con Manzana, which turned out to be sort of a flat apple tart covered by a sugar crust topped with ice cream. We spent a lot of time talking with a young woman, probably the girlfriend of the younger owner whose father had started the brewery.

We went home to bed fairly early after having arranged for a taxi for our early morning boat trip to Buenos Aires.

Fri, Oct 18 - Colonia to Buenos Aires

We woke at 3:50 at a knock on the door. Our taxi arrived at 4:15am. We got to the port by 4:30 only to discover that we had tickets for 5:30 in the afternoon! (We should have noticed 17:30.)

We had to stand at a window for a long time while a very nice woman figured out how to transfer money from one boat company to another (or something).

Finally, with tickets for the boat that was boarding, we checked our bags and went in to Argentinian customs where we were informed that we had to have $160 reciprocity visas, which are sold online!

Fortunately, the kind people working at customs led us back out to an office where they gave us a computer to use to buy the visas online.


Finally we were able to board and go out upon the dark waters towards Buenos Aires.

A few hours later, Buenos Aires came into sight.
