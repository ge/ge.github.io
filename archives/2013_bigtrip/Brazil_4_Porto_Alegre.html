Porto Alegre

Sat Oct 12 - Travel from Florianópolis to Porto Alegre

Dalton woke us up at 7 and took us to a café for coffee and pão com queijo.

At the bus station George found his original bus ticket and got a refund!

After traveling about an hour and a half, the road narrowed for construction but that didn't last long - the road was pretty good.

We had a 30-minute lunch stop at a large bus stop along the toll road. George found some vegetables in a salad bar and ate them. (He didn't pay too dearly for this.)


We got to Porto Alegre 20 minutes late, where Silvia met us and drove us to their condo where we found Ricardo, who had just returned from São Paulo. It was wonderful to see them.

As David's meat mentor, Ricardo drove us to his butcher shop to show David how to buy meat. David was an eager pupil.

When we got back, Ricardo showed David how to grill the meat they had bought. Like most of the houses we had stayed in in Brazil, there was a large open grill in the dining room. Ricardo's grill had pairs of horizontal bars in the back that could hold his skewers by their tips - the skewers, which were unusually strong, stayed fairly horizontal without any support from the front of the grill - it was a very nice system that meant that the skewers had to be turned by hand and they could only turn 180°. But this system produced miracles!




Their son Marcelo and his girlfriend Luisa arrived for dinner. After starting and selling a company, Marcelo is working part time and studying advertising part time. He loves his studies and may go to the US for a Master's degree or even a PhD. We hope he visits us in Chapel Hill/Carrboro. Heck, we hope the whole family visits!







Sun Oct 13 - Craft Fair in Porto Alegre

Ricardo and Silvia took us to downtown to a craft fair in a large park.

Notice the couple with the chimarrão and thermos of hot water. This was a very common sight in Porto Alegre and Montevideo - it's a typical gaúcho custom.







Later we got coffee at the chique Café Barbarella.

We went home to watch soccer. Later in the evening, Ricardo decided to take us to an evening pharmacy, where he got a prescription cough medicine for David and codeine pills for George - somehow George knew he would get sick later on the trip, and codeine is his miracle drug!



Mon Oct 14 - Last day in Porto Alegre

It was a work day, and both Ricardo and Silvia had to go to work. David and George lounged around and then took a taxi to Ricardo's office. We explored out from there, visiting a park, shopping, looking at bicycle signs, etc. Until it was time for lunch with Ricardo.


After lunch, which had lots of vegetables!, we took a long walk to the Mercado Central, where David had a haircut and George used his bad Spanish to wreck his beard.





We took a long walk along the waterfront of the port and then took a taxi home, where we had another wonderful dinner with Silvia, Fernanda and Marcelo.


After dinner Marcelo drove us to the bus station for the overnight bus ride to Montevideo. The bus was not very much fun, but they did give us whiskey and an airline-style dinner.
