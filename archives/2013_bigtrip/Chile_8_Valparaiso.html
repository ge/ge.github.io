Valparaíso, Chile

Sat Nov 9 - Valparaíso

We took an early bus to Valparaíso. Here we are, waiting for it.


Valparaíso turned out to be very dirty and poor. We had read about a garbage strike there but didn't see too many signs of it, thank goodness.

We walked along the bay to Sotomayor Plaza. Along the way we met a very friendly Argentinian man with his daughter. He told us that the funicular operators were on strike but that it was easy to walk up.





Sotomayor Plaza


We looked at cruise ships and lots of tourists and lots of people trying to sell stuff.






Because the funicular workers were on strike, we walked up a very steep narrow walk with lots of steps and lots of graffiti. While George was looking at a cat - thinking about a photo - an older woman asked what he was doing and we started talking. She told us to be careful, as did everyone else we talked to.












We lounged around at the top before deciding to go into a restaurant. We ate fish dishes on a terrace next to a couple from Venice, who proved friendly.





We walked back down and found a taxi to Neruda's house. Unlike his Santiago house, we could wander about on the grounds. We walked down into the yard, thinking we were going to a tourist info place. As we were going down, George asked a woman coming up if there was tourist info below. She said we needed to go look. It was a dead end. The woman came back down and we started talking. She said she lived in Seattle. David said maybe she would know his brother-in-law (she didn't). We found that she worked for a group advocating separate paths for bicycles so George asked if she knew Merlin. Merlin was a friend! David got info to give to Heidi. Her name was Robin Randels, Cascade Bicycle Club.




We walked downhill to a very nice café with Wi-Fi. This brought David the sad but not unexpected news that his beloved Aunt Mavis had died.


We walked down on a very steep and very beautiful street with lots of stairs and painted walls. On the way we met our friends Pablo and Gabriele.








We walked down a very steep and beautiful street to the flat area. We walked through an extensive street fair to the bus station.





Back in Santiago, we walked to the metro and went home for the evening.
