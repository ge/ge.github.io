Bariloche

Mon, Oct 21 - Buenos Aires to San Carlos de Bariloche

David and George spent the morning in the apartment, doing tasks such as making a reservation for that evening in Bariloche. David found a terrific place.

Virginie came by as we were waiting for a car service to arrive to take us to the airport. Good thing, too, because the service had gone to the wrong place and required her help to get to our apartment!

Our plane finally got to Bariloche, a high town next to a large lake and surrounded by towering, snowy mountains.

We checked into our hotel and then wandered around like tourists, eating a wonderful dinner.

This is a quaint bar just down the hill from our hosteleria in Bariloche.




top

Tues, Oct 22 - Walk around Llao Llao

We took a long walk to the bus station to buy tickets to Chile. Then we took a city bus to the end of the line so that we could walk in the fog and drizzle around Llao Llao, a hill on a couple of lakes.

Hiked to top of Llao Llao in occasional light drizzle. It was too cloudy to see snowy peaks or much color.

On the way back, we sat by a lake for lunch and maté. As we sat there, some hikers called George’s name. It was Gina and Toby, Australians that George had sat next to on the plane! We talked and decided to meet for dinner

We walked back and had to run to catch the bus back to town!







All four of us had a nice dinner where we decided to go to Catedral together tomorrow in Toby’s car.

top

Weds, Oct 23 - Hike to Refugio Frey

It was a long hike, about 8 hours with a stay of an hour or so at the refuge itself.

The trail led along a lake and then turned right into a canyon that led to the refuge.

























At the refuge, Luciana Melo, a 34-year old Brazilian, who had walked up alone, asked if she could walk back down with us.







After our long hike down, Luciana went back to her hotel in the ski village. David and George had dinner with Toby and Gina.

top

Thurs, Oct 24 - Bariloche and Cerro Campanario

David and George walked to the Chilean embassy, where we found out that we didn’t need visas to cross over into Chile.

Then we took the city bus to Cerro Campanario, which has a ski lift and a beautiful 360-degree view. We hiked to the top (and took the lift down).



At the top, we spent a lot of time looking at the incomprable view. We were joined by Brian Wright, Canadian violinist who works in London. We ate lunch together in a beautiful round restaurant with an amazing view.










We took the city bus to the intercity bus station in Bariloche to buy tickets to Osorno, Chile. Then we took the long walk back to the part of town near our hotel. We ate dinner and then went back to the hotel for the night.

top

Fri, Oct 25 - El Bolón and Lago Puelo

We rented a car belonging to Mariana, the owner of our hosteleria, and drove to El Bolón and Lago Puelo which was a few kilometers past it. Walked around a bit at the late, then drove back to El Bolón for lunch.

We drove back and then spent the rest of the day doing errands before having dinner in the Juaja restaurant with Toby and Gina. Then we went to bed for our last night in Bariloche.
